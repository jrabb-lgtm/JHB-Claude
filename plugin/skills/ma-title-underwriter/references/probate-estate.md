# Probate & Estate Issues in Massachusetts Title

## TS 14 — Missing Probates

Title from heirs of a decedent with no MA probate is NOT defective if:

**Option A (25-year rule):**
1. Decedent died more than 25 years ago, AND
2. Recorded affidavit or death certificate shows date of death and place of residence at death, AND
3. Affidavit under G.L. c. 183 §§5A or 5B recorded, naming the decedent's heirs, stating decedent died intestate, and declaring no probate proceedings filed in any jurisdiction.

**Option B (50-year rule):**
- Decedent died more than 50 years ago AND instruments in the chain identify the heirs.

**Key notes:**
- Under M.G.L. c. 190B (effective 3/31/2012), probate may generally be opened only within 3 years of death (§3-108).
- A Voluntary Administration under G.L. c. 190, §3-1201 or c. 195, §16 is NOT a probate proceeding for TS 14 purposes.
- When decedent has been dead 25 years without probate, risk of a later will or competing claim is deemed negligible.

**Curative steps for < 25 years with no probate:**
- File probate in MA Probate Court (petition for informal administration under MUPC), OR
- If will exists, probate the will and get personal rep appointed with power of sale.

## TS 36 — Probate Inventories

Title from a decedent's estate is NOT defective by reason of:
- Error in probate inventory identifying the real estate or the decedent's interest,
- Omission of real estate from probate inventory, or
- Absence of a probate inventory entirely.

Under M.G.L. c. 190B, probate inventories are no longer required to be filed with the court in informal or formal proceedings (§§3-306(c) and 3-403(c)), though the personal representative must prepare one within 3 months of appointment.

## TS 41 — List of Heirs

The listing of heirs in probate court proceedings may be relied upon as complete and accurate (absent contrary recorded evidence) if:

**A. Pre-3/31/2012 probate:** Listing on an allowed petition for probate of will or administration (though technically not conclusive per old case law — Cassidy v. Truscott).

**B. Decree and Order:** Court Decree and Order making a determination of heirs (formal or informal).

**C. Form MPC 162 (informal):** Original or duly amended List of Surviving Spouse, Children, Heirs at Law filed with informal petition (MPC 150) that has been allowed, provided it is relied upon AFTER the later of: (a) 12 months from allowance of informal petition, OR (b) 3 years from date of death — AND at least one of:
  1. Decree and Order of Complete Settlement issued (even if it doesn't formally determine heirs), OR
  2. Closing Statement (MPC 850) filed + no challenges within 1 year of filing, OR
  3. Six years have passed since allowance of Bond of Personal Representative.

## TS 71 — Evidence of Death

Title from surviving joint owners or remainderpersons (after life tenant death) is NOT defective as to uncertainty of death if evidence established by:

**(a) Death certificate** recorded at the Registry of Deeds in the district where the property is located, OR filed with/noted in a MA Probate Court docket (any county).

**(b)(1) Certified copy of allowed/approved probate petition** or certificate of appointment, reciting date of death — recording at registry not necessary if petition filed in any MA county.

**(b)(2) MA Inheritance Tax Lien Release** (L-8 or L-53) relative to decedent's interest.

**(b)(3) MA Certificate of Release of Estate Tax Lien** (formerly M-792) relative to decedent's interest.

**(b)(4) Deed from Survivors** with recital that decedent died — even without date or place of death — **if recorded more than 20 years ago**.

**(b)(5) Survivor's death certificate** listing marital status as "widowed" identifying the predeceased joint owner as spouse.

**(b)(6) Attorney affidavit under c. 183 §5B** (when death certificate unavailable due to confidentiality/unavailability) stating: (i) death certificate cannot be obtained + supporting narrative, AND (ii) attaches copy of published obituary with date of death — but only when a prior deed from Survivors to an arm's-length purchaser for value already exists.

**Key note:** An Estate Tax Affidavit (c. 65C §14(a)) is NOT sufficient evidence of death for TS 71 purposes.

## TS 78 — Personal Representative Conveyances Under MUPC

Title from a personal representative appointed under MUPC informal testacy proceeding (G.L. c. 190B §3-301 et seq.) is NOT defective if conveyance made:

**I. Pursuant to informally probated will containing power to sell real estate** (G.L. c. 190B §3-715(23½)); OR

**II. Pursuant to a license to sell real estate** granted under G.L. c. 202.

---

## TS 40 — Transfers by Devisees Under Will Containing Power of Sale

If a will contains a power of sale, devisees who receive property under the will may convey marketable title directly — personal representative does not need to convey.

## Practice Tips for Common Estate Scenarios

### Scenario: Decedent died, joint tenant or tenants-by-entirety survived
→ Record death certificate at registry + check TS 3 (federal estate tax) and TS 24 (MA estate tax).

### Scenario: Decedent was sole owner, no probate, died 30+ years ago
→ TS 14 cure: record (1) death certificate/affidavit of date of death, and (2) §5B affidavit identifying heirs, stating intestacy and no probate.

### Scenario: Decedent was sole owner, no probate, died 10 years ago
→ Need to open MA probate. Determine if will exists. Appoint personal representative with power of sale. Get license from probate court if no power of sale in will.

### Scenario: Estate has been informally probated but no decree determining heirs
→ Use TS 41(C) analysis — check timing thresholds before relying on MPC 162 heir list.
