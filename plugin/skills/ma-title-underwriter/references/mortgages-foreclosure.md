# Mortgages, Foreclosure, Homestead & Related Liens

## TS 25 — Mortgage Discharges

A mortgage may be discharged by an **acknowledged and recorded deed of release or written acknowledgment of payment or satisfaction** executed by:
- The **holder of record**, OR
- **Any one** of joint holders of record — UNLESS they hold as tenants by the entirety.

**Tenants by the entirety:** Both must execute the discharge. However, if only one has executed it, it may still be effective under M.G.L. c. 183 §54(a).

**Tenants in common:** All must join in the discharge (but mortgagees are presumed to hold as joint tenants per M.G.L. c. 184 §7 and Park v. Parker).

**Risk of paying wrong person:** One who satisfies a mortgage by paying the record holder assumes the risk of paying the wrong person unless the holder surrenders the note. (Lamson & Co. v. Abrams, 305 Mass. 238)

## TS 58 — Out-of-Order Recording of Mortgage Discharges and Assignments

Title is NOT defective by reason of:

**1. Discharge recorded before assignment:** Discharge executed by mortgagee who holds record title at the time of discharge, even if a subsequent recording of an assignment by that mortgagee to a third party is later found — regardless of whether the assignment was dated before or after the discharge.

**2. Discharge recorded before mortgagee had record title:** Discharge executed by mortgagee who did NOT hold record title at the time of discharge — if assignments of mortgage to the discharging mortgagee (whenever executed, whether before or after the discharge) are subsequently recorded.

**3. Post-foreclosure assignment recorded:** Recording after foreclosure of an assignment executed prior to first publication of foreclosure sale notice (c. 244 §14), or a confirmatory assignment of a valid prior written assignment, where the mortgage has been foreclosed of record by the assignee. (See U.S. Bank v. Ibanez, 458 Mass. 637 (2011) — assignment must precede or be contemporaneous with foreclosure; confirmatory assignments are valid)

## TS 56 — Mortgage Foreclosures After December 31, 1990 (SCRA)

Title from a mortgage foreclosure **commenced after 12/31/1990** is NOT defective if:

### Part I: SCRA Compliance (one of the following)

**(I)(1) SCRA judgment obtained**, OR

**(I)(2) Record owner (30 days before foreclosure sale) was an Exempt Entity:**
- Corporation (domestic or foreign)
- Limited partnership
- Massachusetts business trust (c. 182)
- Partnership/JV of which all general partners of record are Exempt Entities

(Applies if commenced after 12/31/1990)

**(I)(3) Record owner was** (if commenced after 6/28/1998):
- Limited liability company
- Limited liability partnership
- Any Exempt Entity above

**Caveat:** If an Exempt Entity transfers to a non-Exempt Entity before the foreclosure sale, an SCRA judgment may be required.

**"Date of commencement":** For exempt entity — date first notice of sale sent out (c. 244). For non-exempt entity — date of filing complaint to foreclose.

### Part II: Notice Requirements (one of the following)

**(II)(1) Notice of sale + mortgagee's affidavit** (complying with M.G.L. c. 244 §14) required under M.G.L. c. 244 §15 were recorded, OR

**(II)(2) Three years** have elapsed since a Memorandum or Certificate of open and peaceable entry to foreclosure was recorded per M.G.L. c. 244 §2.

### Common Fatal Defects in Foreclosure Chain
- Individual mortgagor, no SCRA judgment → 🔴 fatal
- Notice of sale not recorded, affidavit missing → 🔴 fatal
- Assignment of mortgage recorded after foreclosure sale, not confirmatory → potentially curable under TS 58(3) if valid written assignment predated first publication

## TS 77 — Prior Owner Automatic or Declared Homestead

Title from a conveyance where there was an automatic or declared homestead is NOT defective if any of:

**1.** Only persons entitled to homestead were a married couple AND **both executed** the deed to bona fide purchaser for value.

**2.** Unmarried person (or their executor/administrator) recited in deed:
- Pre-3/16/2011: "he/she was unmarried"
- On/after 3/16/2011: "he/she was unmarried and no other persons entitled to homestead protection"

**3.** Non-titled spouse **released homestead** in the conveyance deed or separately recorded instrument.

**4.** Recorded §5B affidavit certifying that a subsequent declaration of homestead on other real estate by the prior owner(s) is attached.

**5.** Property held in trust — trustee(s) conveyed to bona fide purchaser for value.

**6.** Deed recites that: (a) property was not homestead property, OR (b) property was not the principal residence of the grantor or any other person.

**7.** Principal residence **abandoned** by all persons entitled to homestead.

**Key notes:**
- Automatic homestead (G.L. c. 188) has been in effect since 3/16/2011 for principal residences.
- Marital status of grantor may be relied upon per G.L. c. 188 §13 — §5B affidavit from grantor is conclusive.
- Two successive deeds to bona fide purchasers for value may constitute abandonment of a prior homestead.
- For trustee-held property, no homestead issue — trustees are not natural persons with principal residences in their capacity as trustees.

## TS 86 — MassHealth Liens and Notices of Claim

MassHealth (Medicaid) files a **Notice of Claim** or **lien** against real property of recipients as recovery for benefits paid. These are recorded at the Registry of Deeds.

**If a MassHealth Notice of Claim or lien is of record:**
- Title is encumbered until the lien is released by the MA Executive Office of Health and Human Services (EOHHS) / MassHealth.
- Contact MassHealth Estate Recovery at (800) 624-6765 to determine amount owed and obtain release.
- The release must be recorded at the registry.

**Estate recovery rules:** MassHealth may seek recovery from the estate of a deceased recipient who was 55 or older when receiving benefits. The lien typically attaches at death and must be paid from the estate or on sale of the property.

## Practical Quick Reference: Mortgage/Encumbrance Issues

| Issue | Standard | Cure |
|-------|---------|------|
| Open mortgage, record holder discharged | TS 25 | Acceptable — discharged by record holder |
| Open mortgage, out-of-order discharge/assignment | TS 58 | Check whether assignments fill the gap |
| Open mortgage, undischarged, holder unknown | TS 25 | Demand letter under c. 183 §54C/55; attorney affidavit of discharge |
| Foreclosure, individual owner, no SCRA judgment | TS 56 | Fatal — obtain SJC relief or title insurance |
| Foreclosure, LLC/corp owner, no SCRA judgment | TS 56(I)(2-3) | Not needed — exempt entity |
| Foreclosure, notice of sale not recorded | TS 56(II) | Fatal — or 3-year open/peaceable entry |
| Post-foreclosure assignment found | TS 58(3) | Acceptable if valid written assignment predated first notice |
| Homestead not released, married couple both signed | TS 77(1) | Acceptable |
| Homestead not released, unmarried grantor no recital | TS 77(2) | Need §5B affidavit from grantor re marital status |
| MassHealth notice of claim recorded | TS 86 | Obtain EOHHS release and record it |
| Private restriction violation, 6+ years | TS 57 | Acceptable — §5B affidavit documenting completion date |
| Private restriction violation, under 6 years | TS 57 | Live risk — flag; may need title insurance endorsement |
