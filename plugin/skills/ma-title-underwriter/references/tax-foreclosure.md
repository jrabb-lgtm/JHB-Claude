# Tax Titles, Land Court Foreclosure & Municipal Liens

## TS 4 — Tax Titles

Title from a tax collector's deed or tax taking instrument is NOT defective if ALL of:

**(1) Proper person was assessed** — the assessed party had an interest of record on assessment day that warranted the assessment under the applicable statute.

**(2) Description sufficient to convey title.**

**(3) One of the following:**

### Path A — Land Court Decree

**(3)(a)(i) Decree of foreclosure (M.G.L. c. 60 §69) or decree establishing title (§80B) has been recorded AND either:**
- Decree is for an unoccupied/abandoned building per §§1 and 81B, OR a §81B certificate is recorded that redemption amount exceeds assessed value, AND **90 days** have elapsed with no petition to vacate — provided record title does not stand in name of deceased person or person under guardianship; OR
- **One year** has elapsed after decree entry with no petition to vacate; OR
- (For deeds dated on/after **November 1, 2024**) Deed from judgment holder transferred title to a good faith purchaser for value.

### Path B — Treasurer's Deed (20-Year Rule)

**(3)(b) More than 20 years** have elapsed since recording of a treasurer's deed under M.G.L. c. 60 §79 or §80, AND no suit alleging noncompliance was commenced and noted in the registry within that period.

### Even Before Waiting Period Expires

A tax title satisfying the above is also NOT defective even before the waiting period if either:
- **(a)** Notice published per court order, OR
- **(b)** All interested parties of record who failed to appear or waive notice received actual notice (postal return receipt signed by interested person or agent = proof of receipt).

### Notes
- Titles not conforming to TS 4 may still be good upon further examination (e.g., low-value taking per M.G.L. c. 60 §79, or Guaranty Mortgage Corp. v. Burlington, 385 Mass. 411 (1982)).
- Assessment to "persons unknown" is valid where property owner is unknown — not a defect under Hardy v. Jaeckle, 371 Mass. 573 (1976).

## TS 80 — Petition to Foreclose Right of Redemption (Open Petition, Redeemed)

Title is NOT defective because of an **open recorded Notice of Petition** to foreclose right of redemption by a municipality (c. 60 §65) where no final decree (§69) has been entered, IF:

- The tax title has NOT been subsequently sold or assigned of record to a third party, AND
- A recorded **instrument of redemption** from the municipality acknowledging satisfaction of the tax is in the record.

**Key caveat:** If the tax title was assigned to a third party (not the municipality) who then filed the foreclosure petition, the municipality cannot unilaterally allow redemption after petition is filed — only the court has jurisdiction (Ithaca Finance, LLC v. Lopez, 95 Mass. App. Ct. 241 (2019)). In that case, a recorded redemption does NOT cure the open petition.

## TS 18 — Municipal Liens

Municipal liens for real estate taxes, water, sewer, and betterments have priority over all private liens. A municipal lien certificate (TS 19) is the proper way to verify outstanding municipal obligations.

**For title purposes:**
- A recorded lien release from the municipality clears the lien.
- Outstanding municipal liens that have not been liened or foreclosed generally do not affect marketability but will need to be paid at closing.
- A tax taking that has not been redeemed and has not been foreclosed keeps the municipality's lien alive indefinitely until redeemed or foreclosed.

---

## TS 19 — Municipal Lien Certificates

A municipal lien certificate under M.G.L. c. 60 §23 should be obtained at or before closing. It discloses:
- Unpaid real estate taxes
- Water and sewer charges
- Betterments
- Other municipal charges

The certificate creates a definitive statement of municipal obligations as of the date of issuance but does not release the obligations — they must be paid at or before closing.

---

## TS 22 — Municipal Betterments

Betterment assessments (road, sewer, water main improvements assessed against abutting properties) are liens on the land. Verify through the municipal lien certificate and pay or apportion at closing. Betterments may be apportioned over up to 20 years — check whether any apportioned betterments remain outstanding.

## MA Land Court Tax Foreclosure Process (Practical Guide)

### How Tax Foreclosure Works in MA

1. **Tax taking** — Municipality records taking with registry of deeds (c. 60 §53).
2. **Petition to foreclose** — Municipality (or assignee) files petition in Land Court to foreclose right of redemption (c. 60 §65).
3. **Notice** — All interested parties must receive notice per Land Court order.
4. **Decree** — Land Court enters final decree foreclosing right of redemption (c. 60 §69) — this is what makes title marketable under TS 4(3)(a).
5. **Waiting period** — 1 year (standard) or 90 days (abandoned/unoccupied) after decree before title is clean under TS 4.

### Common Title Problems with Tax Titles

| Problem | Analysis |
|---------|----------|
| No Land Court decree and less than 20 years from treasurer's deed | Fatal — title not marketable |
| Decree entered but less than 1 year old | Review for 90-day abandoned building path; otherwise wait or obtain court documents |
| Decree entered, 1+ years, no vacate petition | Marketable under TS 4(3)(a)(ii) |
| Taking assigned to third party — open petition, recorded redemption | NOT cured — Ithaca Finance applies |
| Taking by municipality — open petition, recorded redemption | Cured under TS 80 |
| Treasurer's deed 20+ years, no suit | Marketable under TS 4(3)(b) |
| Assessed to wrong person | Potentially fatal — analyze whether person had interest of record |
| Insufficient description | Potentially fatal — depends on whether description can convey |
