# Federal & Massachusetts Estate Tax Liens

## TS 3 — Federal Estate Tax Liens

The federal estate tax lien (IRC §6324(a)(1)) arises automatically at death if estate tax is owed, and attaches for **10 years from date of death**. It is distinct from the general recorded federal tax lien (IRC §6321).

Title is NOT defective by reason of a federal estate tax lien if ANY of the following apply:

**(1) Affidavit that gross estate is below the exclusion threshold for the year of death** — signed by personal representative, executor, administrator, or (if no probate) immediate successor in title or person in possession. Use REBA Form 32.

**(2) Tax closing letter** showing amount paid or zero due.

**(3) IRS account transcript** with transaction code 421 and explanation "Closed examination of tax return" — functional equivalent of closing letter (IRS Notice 2017-12).

**(4) IRS certificate of discharge, release, or non-attachment** (IRC §6325).

**(5) Transfer to a "purchaser" or "security interest" holder** (IRC §6323(h)) for non-probate property, in an arm's-length transaction — death after 11/2/1966.

**(6) 10 years from date of death** — lien expires automatically.

### Federal Exclusion Thresholds by Year of Death

| Year of Death | Gross Estate Threshold |
|--------------|----------------------|
| 2006–2008 | $2,000,000 |
| 2009 | $3,500,000 |
| 2010 | $5,000,000 (see note) |
| 2011 | $5,000,000 |
| 2012 | $5,120,000 |
| 2013 | $5,250,000 |
| 2014 | $5,340,000 |
| 2015 | $5,430,000 |
| 2016 | $5,450,000 |
| 2017 | $5,490,000 |
| 2018+ | $11,180,000+ (indexed for inflation — check IRC §2010(c)(3) for current year) |

**Note on 2010 deaths:** Personal representative could elect modified carryover basis (no estate tax) OR pay estate tax with $5M exclusion. Absent evidence of election out, presume $5M exclusion applies and require release if estate exceeded threshold.

**Key:** These thresholds are reduced by adjusted taxable gifts made after 1976 and specific gift tax exemptions. Gross estate includes joint property, life insurance, trust funds, and other non-probate assets — a small probate estate does NOT mean no lien.

**Where to record:** Documents under (1)-(3) relate to value/status of assets, not a specific property — need only be recorded in any county where decedent owned property (not every county). Can be recorded in unregistered land system even if decedent's only property is registered land.

## TS 24 — Massachusetts Estate Tax Liens

MA estate tax is **separate** from federal after 1/1/2003 ("decoupled"). Lien lasts **10 years from date of death**.

### 24.1 — Deaths Before January 1, 1997

No MA estate tax lien if MA net estate was below:

| Year of Death | Net Estate Threshold |
|--------------|---------------------|
| Prior to 1993 | $200,000 |
| 1993 | $300,000 |
| 1994 | $400,000 |
| 1995 | $500,000 |
| 1996 | $600,000 |

MA net estate = MA gross estate minus funeral expenses, claims, mortgages, and debts (M.G.L. c. 65C §1(g)).

**Cure (if estate above threshold or unclear):**
1. Proof of payment shown by MA estate tax closing letter (property must be in probate inventory or, for non-probate property, documented in tax return), OR
2. Commissioner of Revenue certificate of release or partial discharge of lien.

### 24.2 — Deaths January 1, 1997 through December 31, 2002

No MA estate tax lien if sum of (federal taxable estate + adjusted taxable gifts) was below:

| Year of Death | Filing Threshold |
|--------------|-----------------|
| 1997 | $600,000 |
| 1998 | $625,000 |
| 1999 | $650,000 |
| 2000, 2001 | $675,000 |
| 2002 | $1,000,000 |

**Cure:**
1. MA estate tax closing letter showing payment, OR
2. Commissioner of Revenue certificate of release, OR
3. Affidavit under M.G.L. c. 65C §§14(a) and 6(a) stating estate does not require filing a federal estate tax return. (REBA Form 32)

### 24.3 — Deaths January 1, 2003 and After

MA estate tax decoupled from federal — tied to IRC as of 12/31/2000.

No MA estate tax lien if sum of (federal taxable estate + adjusted taxable gifts) was below:

| Year of Death | Filing Threshold |
|--------------|-----------------|
| 2003 | $700,000 |
| 2004 | $850,000 |
| 2005 | $950,000 |
| 2006 and after | $1,000,000 |

**Note:** For 2024+, the MA threshold was raised to $2,000,000 by St. 2023, c. 50. Always verify current threshold against current MA DOR guidance.

**Cure:**
1. MA estate tax closing letter showing payment (property in inventory or documented in tax return for non-probate), OR
2. Commissioner of Revenue certificate of release, OR
3. Affidavit under penalties of perjury that estate does not require filing a MA estate tax return based on the applicable threshold (M.G.L. c. 65C §§14(a) and 6(a) — REBA Form 32).

**Caveat:** Gross estate includes life insurance, joint property, trust funds, and other non-probate assets. Small probate estate ≠ no MA estate tax lien.

### 24.4 — Gifts in Contemplation of Death

Property free of lien from "gift in contemplation of death" on subsequent transfer to bona fide purchaser for adequate consideration if:
1. Conveyed by deed not disclosing intent to take effect at/after death, AND
2. Deed recorded prior to decedent's death. (M.G.L. c. 65C §14(b))

## Practical Cure Chart

| Situation | Cure Document |
|-----------|--------------|
| Death 10+ years ago (federal) | No action needed — lien expired |
| Death 10+ years ago (MA) | No action needed — lien expired |
| Estate clearly below threshold | REBA Form 32 affidavit (federal) + §14(a) affidavit (MA) |
| Estate size uncertain | Request IRS closing letter + MA DOR closing letter |
| Already paid | Obtain proof of payment letter; record REBA Form 32A |
| Joint tenancy/survivorship | Lien may not attach to survivor's interest if arm's-length transfer; still check thresholds |
